# Zambia Payments & Compliance Verification Brief — July 2026

*Method note: direct page fetches were egress-blocked in this environment; claims were verified via search-indexed content of the cited pages, cross-checked across multiple sources where possible. [verified] = operator's own page or multiple independent sources; [likely] = single/secondary source; [unclear] = unpublished or conflicting.*

## 1. Payment Aggregators Serving Zambia

**Flutterwave** — Operates in Zambia under a Payment System Licence from the Bank of Zambia, announced Feb 2025 (https://flutterwave.com/ng/blog/zambia-here-we-come-flutterwave-receives-license-to-power-payments-for-zambian-businesses) [verified]. Rails: MTN MoMo, Airtel Money, and Zamtel mobile money, plus cards, Apple Pay/Google Pay (https://developer.flutterwave.com/v3.0/docs/zambia-mobile-money; https://flutterwave.com/gb/support/payment-methods/pay-with-mobile-money) [verified]. Fees: ~2% local transactions (1.4% transaction + 0.6% platform), 4.8% international (https://flutterwave.com/us/support/pricing/pricing-for-receiving-payment) [likely]. Settlement: local settlements within 24h; foreign in ~5 days; mobile money refunds 1–7 days (https://flutterwave.com/us/support/pricing/pricing-for-receiving-payment) [likely]. KYC: supports unregistered business, registered business (sole prop/LLC) and NGO account tiers; Zambian registered-business accounts need business registration docs, ZRA tax registration (TPIN), bank statement matching the trading name, and director ID with live capture (https://flutterwave.com/gb/support/my-account/onboarding-requirements-for-using-flutterwave-in-zambia) [likely]. API: full public docs, sandbox, webhooks, hosted checkout + direct charge (https://developer.flutterwave.com/v3.0/docs/zambia-mobile-money) [verified].

**DPO Pay (DPO Group / Network International)** — Active in Zambia with a Lusaka office; supports Visa, Mastercard, Amex, Diners + MTN and Airtel mobile money; ZMW supported (https://dpogroup.com/online-payments/zambia/) [verified]. Zamtel Kwacha support not confirmed on their Zambia page [unclear]. Fees: not published — no setup/monthly fees, transaction fee negotiated per merchant, VAT added on fees (https://dpogroup.com/faq/; https://dpogroup.com/blog/payment-processing-fees-merchants-should-expect-pay/) [verified-as-unpublished]. Settlement: to nominated bank account; a "Real-Time Settlement" product exists, standard cadence unpublished (https://www.dpogroup.com/africa/real-time-settlements/) [unclear]. Onboarding: KYC docs, account claimed live within 24h; business registration number requested, national ID accepted for unregistered sellers on some partner platforms (https://dpogroup.com/faq/; https://helpcenter.dizlog.com/en/articles/7994354-activate-dpo-payment-gateway) [likely]. API: public docs + plugins (Shopify/Woo), long-standing e-commerce orientation (https://dpogroup.com/online-payments/) [likely].

**Pesapal** — Dedicated Zambia operation (https://www.pesapal.com/zm) [verified]. Rails: mobile money + Visa/Mastercard (Amex over the counter) (https://www.pesapal.com/zm/business/online) [verified]. Fees: 3.5% per transaction, no setup/monthly fees, custom rates for high volume (https://www.pesapal.com/zm/business/pricing) [verified]. Settlement: funds held in trust account, settled on request within 72 working hours / max 3 working days (https://www.pesapal.com/zm/business/faqs) [verified]. KYC: merchant agreement + documents varying by company/sole-prop/partnership type (https://www.pesapal.com/zm/business/faqs) [verified]. API: public API 3.0 docs + plugins (https://www.pesapal.com/zm/business/online/api-plugins) [verified].

**Lenco / BroadPay** — Lenco is the flagship gateway of BroadPay Zambia Ltd, a BoZ-licensed Payment System Business (Licence 02/PSB/2015) (https://lenco.co/zm; https://www.broadpayzm.com/) [verified]. Rails: MTN MoMo, Airtel Money, Zamtel Kwacha, Visa/Mastercard, bank transfer (https://lenco.co/zm) [verified]. Fees: 3.5% on card and mobile-money collections; volume discounts above K5M/month (https://lenco.co/zm/pricing) [likely]. Onboarding: digital business account opening, local support in Lusaka; exact document list not published [unclear]. API: public API for collections, payouts, and bill payments; GitHub org LencoHQ (https://lenco.co/zm; https://github.com/LencoHQ) [verified].

**PawaPay** — Mobile-money-only aggregator; Zambia covered on all three rails: MTN (MTN_MOMO_ZMB), Airtel (AIRTEL_OAPI_ZMB), Zamtel (ZAMTEL_ZMB) (https://docs.pawapay.io/v2/docs/test_numbers; https://status.pawapay.io/) [verified]. **MTN MoMo Open API** — Zambia is one of ~12 live MoMo API markets (extended 2019–2022) (https://www.ericsson.com/en/cases/2023/mtn-mobile-money-open-apis; https://momo.mtn.com/api/) [verified]. Portal: https://momodeveloper.mtn.com with Collections, Disbursements, Remittances products and a free sandbox (instant subscription keys) (https://momodeveloper.mtn.com/api-documentation) [verified]. Go-live: submit business KYC for the specific country, approval grants credentials on the separate production portal momoapi.mtn.com — no public production signup (https://momoapi.mtn.com/faqs; https://momodevelopercommunity.mtn.com/getting-started-in-the-community-2/how-to-become-a-momo-api-developer-197) [verified]. Fees: not published; commercial terms negotiated with MTN Zambia [unclear]. Registered business + local presence effectively required for production KYC [likely].

**Airtel Money API** — Zambia explicitly supported on the pan-African portal https://developers.airtel.africa (https://developers.airtel.africa/) [verified]. Collections (USSD-push debit) + Disbursements; OAuth2 client-credentials; UAT sandbox (openapiuat.airtel.africa) open, but client_secret/production only released after submitting business formalities/KYC to Airtel (https://medium.com/@muhanzi/how-to-integrate-airtel-money-api-for-payment-collections-and-remittances-67d2202cd488; https://github.com/Chizzoz/airtel-money-expressjs) [likely]. **Zamtel Kwacha** — No public developer portal exists; a pay-bill/checkout REST API (USSD-push) is available only after commercial paperwork with Zamtel's commercial team (https://killianstark.hashnode.dev/how-to-integrate-to-zamtel-mobile-money-pay-billrest-api) [likely]. ## 3. ZRA Compliance — Smart Invoice / VAT / TPIN

- Smart Invoice e-invoicing became mandatory 1 July 2024 for all VAT-registered taxpayers regardless of size/turnover, including non-residents registered for Zambian VAT (via local tax agent) (https://edicomgroup.com/blog/mandatory-electronic-invoicing-zambia-smart-invoice; https://www.zra.org.zm/smart-invoice-learn-more/) [verified].
- Since 1 Jan 2025, input VAT deductions are only allowed on invoices generated through Smart Invoice (https://sovos.com/regulatory-updates/vat/zambia-grace-period-on-e-invoicing-via-smart-invoice-system/; https://www.pagero.com/compliance/regulatory-updates/zambia) [verified].
- Scope extends beyond VAT to Turnover Tax, Rental Income, Insurance Premium Levy, Tourism Levy and Local Excise on electricity; turnover-tax-only businesses may use the free Smart Invoice mobile app instead of desktop/online clients (https://www.zra.org.zm/smart-invoice-learn-more/; https://www.cleartax.com/zm/e-invoicing-zambia) [verified].
- VAT registration threshold: ZMW 800,000 taxable turnover in any 12 months, or ZMW 200,000 in any 3 consecutive months (https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/zambia/corporate/other-taxes) [verified]. Turnover Tax regime (for non-VAT businesses) threshold rose to ZMW 5,000,000 at 5% from Jan 2025, creating an awkward K800k–K5M overlap zone — get local tax advice there (https://mjconsultants.co.zm/zambias-turnover-tax-new-threshold-and-its-impact-on-businesses/) [likely].
- E-commerce: no special carve-out — online sellers are fully subject to normal ZRA rules (TPIN, then turnover tax or VAT + Smart Invoice as applicable) (https://voxforem.org/blog/zra-tax-compliance-for-ecommerce-businesses) [likely].
- Penalties: fines up to ~K120,000 or up to 3 years imprisonment for non-issuance; enforcement began 1 Oct 2024, and ZRA moved to prosecute 7 taxpayers in May 2026 — enforcement is real (https://www.flick.network/en-zm/e-invoicing-in-zambia; https://diggers.news/business/2026/05/12/zra-to-prosecute-7-taxpayers-for-non-use-of-smart-invoice/) [verified].
- Software integration: via the VSDC (Virtual Sales Data Controller) REST API bridging your invoicing system (CIS) to Smart Invoice; public spec PDFs (v1.0.7/1.0.8) on zra.org.zm; process = register on the Smart Invoice Taxpayer Portal → apply for VSDC service → technical/administrative verification → sandbox testing → approval (https://www.zra.org.zm/wp-content/uploads/2024/08/VSDC-API-Specification-Document-v1.0.7-1.pdf; https://www.zra.org.zm/smart-invoice-registration-guide/) [verified].
- TPIN: mandatory for every individual/business transacting with ZRA and a prerequisite for VAT/TOT registration and business bank accounts (https://www.zra.org.zm/business-registration-requirements/; https://www.businesslicenses.gov.zm/business-procedures/details/tpin-registration) [verified].

## 4. WhatsApp & SMS

- WhatsApp Business Platform moved from conversation-based to **per-delivered-template-message pricing on 1 July 2025**; categories: marketing (always charged), utility (free inside an open 24h customer-service window), authentication, service (free). Volume tiers discount utility/auth; all messages free inside 72h free-entry-point windows (https://developers.facebook.com/documentation/business-messaging/whatsapp/pricing; https://help.twilio.com/articles/30304057900699-Notice-Changes-to-WhatsApp-s-Pricing-July-2025) [verified].
- Zambia bills under the "Rest of Africa" market: marketing ≈ US$0.0225/msg; utility ≈ US$0.006/msg after the Nov 2024 reduction; auth in a similar sub-$0.03 band; rates revised quarterly — confirm ZM row on Meta's rate card before budgeting (https://whatsappbusiness.com/products/platform-pricing/; https://docs.askyazi.com/whatsapp-api-pricing/pricing) [likely].
- Unofficial gateways (WAHA, Baileys, Evolution API): reverse-engineered WhatsApp Web clients that violate Meta's ToS; accounts face detection and permanent bans with no reliable appeal — one practitioner dataset puts proactive-messaging ban rates at 15–30%/12 months vs <2% for purely reactive use (https://waha.devlike.pro/; https://blog.kraya-ai.com/whatsapp-automation-ban-risk) [likely]. Unsafe for order-critical notifications on a commercial platform [likely].
- SMS fallback: Africa's Talking covers Zambia (bulk SMS, shortcodes, USSD) from ~ZMW 0.17/SMS (https://africastalking.com/pricing; https://help.africastalking.com/en/articles/4162198-zambia-shortcodes) [likely]; cheaper local direct-connection options exist, e.g. LineServe at ZMW 0.11/SMS across MTN/Airtel/Zamtel (https://www.lineserve.net/messaging/bulk-sms/zambia) [likely].

## 5. Bank of Zambia — Marketplace Collections

- The NPSA 2007 required any "payment system business" (incl. aggregators, money transmitters, e-money issuers) to be designated/licensed by BoZ; requirements and application forms are published (https://www.boz.zm/01ARequirementsforSettingupaPaymentSystemBusiness.pdf; https://www.boz.zm/designation-of-payment-systems.htm) [verified].
- **New law:** the National Payment System Act No. 5 of 2026 (April 2026) repealed and replaced the 2007 Act — it introduces licensing/designation/authorisation of "payment service providers," minimum initial + continuing capital set by BoZ, consumer-protection and market-conduct rules (https://zambialii.org/akn/zm/act/2026/5/eng@2026-04-08; https://www.parliament.gov.zm/node/13028) [verified].
- E-money and mobile payments are further governed by the E-Money Issuance Directives 2018 and the ATM/POS/Internet/Mobile Payments Directives 2019 (https://www.boz.zm/directives.htm; https://comesabusinesscouncil.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/3-National-Payment-Systems-Directives-on-Electronic-Money-Issuance-2018.pdf) [verified].
- There is **no published threshold or explicit exemption** letting a marketplace hold/pool vendor funds unlicensed; a platform that itself collects and holds money on behalf of vendors risks meeting the PSP definition under the 2026 Act, whereas structuring so a BoZ-licensed aggregator (Flutterwave, BroadPay/Lenco, cGrate, etc.) receives, holds and splits/settles funds is the standard compliant route — get Zambian counsel to confirm the escrow/split-settlement structure under the new Act (https://zambialii.org/akn/zm/act/2026/5/eng@2026-04-08; https://www.afriwise.com/blog/regulation-of-fintech-in-zambia---a-legal-guide) [unclear].

**Recommendation.** For a Zambian marketplace, integrate one BoZ-licensed aggregator covering all three mobile-money rails plus cards as the funds-handling layer — Lenco (local, all rails, same-day MoMo settlement) or Flutterwave (stronger API, lower headline fee) are the leading candidates, with PawaPay the best pure mobile-money option if cards can wait. Riding on the licensed aggregator avoids the platform itself needing a PSP licence under the new National Payment System Act 2026, but the vendor-payout/escrow structure should be confirmed with Zambian counsel before launch. In parallel, secure TPIN + VAT/TOT registration and plan a ZRA Smart Invoice VSDC API integration from day one, and use the official WhatsApp Cloud API (service + utility messages are cheap or free) with Africa's Talking SMS as fallback rather than any unofficial WhatsApp gateway.
